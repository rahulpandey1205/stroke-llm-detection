Clinical Protocols & Guidelines

Standardized medical thresholds and preventative strategy reference.

Biomarker Thresholds
Fasting Glucose > 140 mg/dL

Levels consistently above 140 mg/dL (or HbA1c > 6.5%) indicate Hyperglycemia, a major driver of arterial damage.

Body Mass Index (BMI) 25.0 - 29.9 (Overweight)
BMI > 30.0 (Obese)

High BMI contributes to systemic inflammation and sleep apnea, both which elevate stroke risk.

Blood Pressure > 140/90 mmHg

Chronic Hypertension is the single most significant modifiable risk factor for both ischemic and hemorrhagic strokes.

Risk Mitigation Strategies
Smoking Cessation

Quitting smoking reduces stroke risk by 50% within 2 years.

Physical Activity

30 mins of moderate aerobic exercise 5x per week is recommended.

DASH Diet

Emphasis on fruits, vegetables, and low-fat dairy reduces hypertension.

Medication Adherence

Consistent control of blood pressure and glucose via prescribed therapy.
